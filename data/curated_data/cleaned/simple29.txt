

Samak Sundaravej

Samak Sundaravej ( June  -  November ) was the th Prime Minister of Thailand, Minister of Defence and also the leader of People’s Power Party. He was named by the mass media as "Uncle Mak" or "Mr. Rose Apple Nose" because of the shape of his nose. He was forced to resign as prime minister because he was also acting as chef on a TV show. The constitutional court in Thailand said he could not have two jobs.

Sundaravej was the son of Samian Sundaravej and Amphan Jitrakorn. He was born in Bangkok in . He married Lady Surat Sundaravej and they had twins called Kandapha and Kanjanakorn. After finishing university, he began writing articles about politics for ‘Siam government’ and ‘Bangkokian’.

He started in politics as a member of "Democrat Party" in . He became well–known in  from radio managing which has the information that attack education organization in that age. He also encouraged the people to loathe the education organization. He was the one who gave the command to move the student resistant gang on th October .
At the end of that, he became the Minister of Interior, as well as resigning from the Democrat Party. In , he set up the new party and became the leader of ‘Thai Population Party’.

He was elected to be a Bangkok governor in  with ,, votes, which was more votes than ever before. The person who came second was Sudarat Keyuraphan (the member from the Thais Love Thailand Party).

Sundaravej took a position of a governor during -. But after he passed the office, he’d been suspected to relate with the lawsuit about buying fire engines until now. He decided not to apply for a governor, but applied to be a senator instead.

Until th January , the House of Representatives had a resolution to appoint Samak to be Prime Minister. He was agreed to be a Minister of Defence. He was suspected to be Taksin (rd Prime Minister; the one who was fleeing imprison punishment to foreign country)’s nominee.

On th September , Samak resigned from being the leader of the ‘People’s Power Party’ with the reason that he had done his duty, so he wanted to end his political role and let the members decide what to do next.

Sundaravej died at the age of  in Bangkok. He died because of liver cancer, which he had been fighting for one year.
